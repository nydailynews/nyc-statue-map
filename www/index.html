Intro

Total revenue

  • $15.3 billion

    Total MTA revenue

    This number comes from the MTA's 2017 mid-year forecast and includes the revenue sources below.

Where the money comes from

  • Fares

    $6.2 billion

    This is money collected from every MetroCard swipe and ticket punched on the MTA’s two commuter lines, Long Island Rail Road and Metro-North.

  • Dedicated Taxes

    $5.3 billion

    Click to jump to the taxes breakdown

  • Tolls

    $1.9 billion

    Collected from the MTA’s seven bridges and two tunnels

  • State & Local Subsidies

    $1.2 billion

    Click to jump to the subsidies breakdown

  • Other

    $685 million

    This is all operating revenues generated from sources other than fares and tolls, such as advertising, reimbursements for paratransit service, senior and student fares, rents, parking and investment income.

Taxes in detail

  • MMTOA

    $1.7 billion

    Metropolitan Mass Transportation Operating Assistance Fund: This is a pot of tax money collected for the MTA and other downstate transportation systems. It’s made up of revenue, such as statewide tax on petroleum companies, a sales tax in the 12-county MTA region and corporate franchise taxes and fees both statewide and in the MTA region. MMTOA this year will collect a total of $2,318m - 72% of which goes to the MTA.

  • Petroleum Business Tax

    $613 million

    This is a statewide tax on petroleum businesses, plus taxes on gas and diesel fuel and fees for vehicle registrations and driver licenses.

  • Mortgage Recording

    $444 million

    A tax on mortgage borrowers and lenders in the 12-county MTA region

  • Urban Tax

    $574 million

    This is a tax collected on property transactions and mortgage recordings in New York City.

  • Payroll Mobility Tax

    $1.5 billion

    This is a tax created during the 2009 MTA bailout on employers and self-employed individuals in the MTA region.

  • Payroll Mobility Tax replacement funds

    $244 million

    In 2011, the politically unpopular tax got slashed, with Gov. Cuomo promising the state will make up the shortfall to make the MTA whole. In last year’s budget, however, $65 million from this reimbursement was cut.

  • MTA Aid

    $305 million

    This is a hodgepodge of new fees from 2009 that includes a 50-cent fee on city taxi trips, a $25 annual fee on motor vehicle registrations, auto rentals and licenses.
